Part 1 - Interview

Introduction (4-5 minutes)

The examiner will ask general questions about familiar topics. Give extended answers with reasons and examples.

Part 1 timer: ⏱ 4:30

Teachers: pause between questions to explain vocabulary. The deck is planned so an unpaused run lasts about 14 minutes.

Part 1 Question 1

1. Do you have homework every day?

Follow-up: How long does it take?
Model Answer
Yes, I have homework every day. It usually takes about one hour.
Part 1 Question 2

2. Do you have time to play?

Follow-up: When?
Model Answer
Yes, I play after I finish my homework. I also play at school during break time.
Part 1 Question 3

3. Do you think children are too busy?

Follow-up: Why?
Model Answer
Some children are too busy because they have school, homework, and many after-school classes. They need more time to play.
Part 2 - Long Turn

Individual Long Turn (3-4 minutes)

You will receive a cue card with a topic. You have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 1-2 minutes.

Tip: Use all the prompts on the card. Structure your answer: past -> present -> future. Do not memorise scripted answers - examiners can tell.

Part 2 - Cue Card

A Busy Day You Had

  • When it was
  • What you did
  • Why you were busy
  • And explain how you felt at the end
Model Answer
I would like to talk about a busy day I had last week. I had school in the morning, then a football match, and then a birthday party. I was busy because I had many things in one day. At the end I felt tired but happy because I had fun with my friends.
Part 3 - Discussion

Two-Way Discussion (4-5 minutes)

The examiner asks more abstract, in-depth questions related to the Part 2 topic.

Tip: Give balanced opinions. Acknowledge both sides before giving your own view. Use phrases like "On one hand... on the other hand..."

Part 3 Question 1

1. Is it good for children to be busy?

Follow-up: Why?
Model Answer
It is good to have some activities, but children also need free time to rest and play.
Part 3 Question 2

2. Do you like weekends?

Follow-up: Why?
Model Answer
Yes, I like weekends because I do not have school and I can spend time with my family.
Part 3 Question 3

3. What do you do when you are tired?

Follow-up: Why?
Model Answer
When I am tired, I sleep or watch cartoons because they help me relax.
Key Takeaways

Summary

  • Part 1: Give full answers with reasons and examples
  • Part 2: Cover all the points on the cue card
  • Part 3: Share your opinion and explain it clearly
  • Golden rule: Keep speaking naturally and fluently
